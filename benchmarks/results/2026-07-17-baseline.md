## Executive summary

Karta's execution flow is feature-complete and field-proven. Two consumer repos delivered 21 binders / 54 work
items with zero production failures of the retry ladder: no failed refs, no kickbacks, no human escalations
anywhere in field history. Adoption of new features, when the consumer was active, was same-day fast — parchmark
filed settings binders 45 minutes after 1.8.0 and enabled kaizen 30 minutes after 1.13.0.

The framework's integrity, however, rests on prose an LLM can skip. The subsystem map counts ~107 prose-only
rules against ~81 scripted checks (benchmarks/flow/seed-map-2026-07-17.json), and the field evidence shows
exactly those prose seams slipping: parchmark's backend-hygiene delivery dropped the commit-marker convention
entirely (0/5 items marked), gringotts hand-edited a committed binder past the immutability doctrine (1f548e5),
doc-gardner skipped two eligible parchmark deliveries (#127/#128), and kaizen's one field pack edit was rescoped
by a human 17 minutes later (dd80e01 → 08bc02a). Deliver is the worst subsystem at 9/25 entries enforced (~36%):
all four core merge invariants are prose-only. Hardening velocity is ~1 rule per release. The bench exists to
turn that backlog into a measured, blocking burndown instead of a decades-long tail.

## Corrections of record

The bench's first lesson is about its own inputs. Two claims produced from karta-repo-only evidence were refuted
the moment the consumer repos were read.

**(a) "Integration oracle never used (0/23)" — FALSE in the field.** The seed map's evidence-subsystem gap
states: "The 'integration' oracle type has never been used: across all 23 archived work items the distribution
is smoke 15, unit 5, e2e 3, integration 0." That is true only of karta's own dogfood archive. The corrected
distribution:

| corpus | items | unit | integration | e2e | smoke |
|-|-|-|-|-|-|
| karta archive (dogfood) | 23 | 5 | 0 | 3 | 15 |
| parchmark | 40 | 19 | 19 | 0 | 2 |
| gringotts | 14 | 4 | 9 | 1 | 0 |
| consumers combined | 54 | 23 | 28 | 1 | 2 |
| all three | 77 | 28 | 28 | 4 | 17 |

Integration is the single most-used oracle type in the field (28/54, 52% of consumer items). Karta-on-karta
binders skew smoke because a plugin repo's "acceptance" is usually its own integrity suite (13 of 15 smoke
commands are the identical repo-gates invocation). Lesson: the bench must always read the consumer repos — a
self-hosted corpus is systematically unrepresentative. This is why the field-* family exists.

**(b) Mid-flight gringotts snapshot was stale within minutes.** benchmarks/fixtures/REGISTRY.json:8 annotates
the vendored browse-refinements binder as a "mid-delivery snapshot: item-wide-screen-layout built-but-not-done."
Field evidence shows item-wide-screen-layout has BOTH built and done refs (2ed282d, written 20:01:05, ~6 minutes
before inspection). The genuinely unfinished item is item-multi-tag-filter: branch parked at wave-2-base
(0c2aa9b) with zero commits and a live worktree. Lesson: mid-flight state annotations rot in minutes; the bench
must re-derive delivery state at run time and stamp provenance notes with an inspection timestamp, never trust a
vendored annotation. The REGISTRY line needs the correction applied.

No other seed-map claim was contradicted by field-evidence.json; the seed map makes no other consumer-repo
assertions (0 mentions of either repo), which is itself the gap that correction (a) documents.
